Unit 3: Narrative Moments

This unit focuses on telling emotional stories through the eyes of animals. Through quiet scenes and specific framing, these images explore themes of solitude, curiosity, and companionship from a non-human perspective.

Twilight Crossing

A lone horse walks into the fading light, symbolizing transition and independence.

Composition: Rule of thirds places the horse off-center for balance and depth.

Lighting: Natural backlighting creates a silhouette effect and a dramatic sunset tone.

Element of Design: Line—pathway and horizon lead the viewer’s eye inward.

Principle of Design: Movement—viewers are visually pulled forward with the horse.

WHAT: A silhouette image of a horse walking away under twilight colors, alone on an open path.

HOW: I used my phone to capture the horse against a low sunset using manual exposure for dramatic contrast and depth.

WHY: This piece reflects themes of independence and leaving behind familiarity—viewed from an animal’s narrative perspective.

Rain Watcher

A cat quietly observes the world through a rain-speckled window, evoking stillness and thought.

Composition: Centered subject within square framing creates symmetry and calm.

Lighting: Diffused window light softens shadows and brings detail to the glass.

Element of Design: Value—light and shadow create emotional tone.

Principle of Design: Emphasis—the contrast between cat and background draws the eye.

WHAT: A quiet moment of my cat watching raindrops fall from the inside of a window, lost in thought.

HOW: Shot using my phone in portrait mode with natural light from behind the glass, with the window creating a filter of mood.

WHY: This piece symbolizes reflection, waiting, and calmness—emotions we often share with animals but never express aloud.

Hidden Pause

A cautious cat hides under a table, capturing a moment of tension and awareness.

Composition: Framed by furniture edges to guide the viewer into the subject’s space.

Lighting: Side lighting adds dimensionality and shadows to the corners.

Element of Design: Space—the open floor creates negative space around the subject.

Principle of Design: Contrast—between light/hidden, soft/focused, cat/floor.

WHAT: A low-angle capture of my cat under the table in a moment of pause and alertness.

HOW: I placed the camera low and used my phone’s wide-angle setting, focusing on space and tension created by shadows.

WHY: The work shows hesitation and vulnerability from a pet’s view—reminding us that even the quietest beings can feel uncertainty.
