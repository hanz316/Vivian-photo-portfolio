Unit 2: New Perspectives

This unit embraces creative reinterpretation and abstract storytelling. Using digital editing and mixed media, I explored themes of identity, awareness, and sustainability through visual experimentation.

Screened Reality

A digital collage mixing pandemic visuals and personal objects to reflect isolation and global awareness.

Composition: Layered montage offers chaos and cohesion simultaneously.

Lighting: Artificial composited light simulates news-like urgency.

Element of Design: Shape—cutouts overlap to form complexity.

Principle of Design: Contrast—sharp edges vs. soft color fields convey tension.

WHAT: I created a collage of pandemic-related symbols such as masks and digital screens blended with hand-drawn icons and textures.

HOW: I used free mobile collage apps to compile images and adjust layer opacity and texture filters. All assets were collected digitally.

WHY: This was my response to digital burnout and emotional overload during lockdowns, visually representing chaos within information.

Dual Self

A split-tone portrait visualizing the psychological duality of personal identity.

Composition: Symmetry through mirrored halves emphasizes tension.

Lighting: Colored gels cast opposing tones across the face.

Element of Design: Color—red and blue dominate the emotional tone.

Principle of Design: Emphasis—the face remains central and confronting.

WHAT: A stylized self-portrait that splits my face into warm and cool tones.

HOW: I used my phone camera and two colored flashlights to create contrast. I edited color balance in Snapseed.

WHY: It represents the inner tension of emotion vs. logic, and how identity is never just one side.

Reflective Portal

A surreal view through a cat's eye offers new perspective on scale and environment.

Composition: Extreme close-up magnifies details and distortion.

Lighting: Back lighting enhances reflection and clarity in the eye.

Element of Design: Texture—fur and glassy eye surface stand in contrast.

Principle of Design: Focus—only the pupil is sharp, directing attention.

WHAT: A macro image of my cat’s eye with a distorted view of the room inside.

HOW: I used my phone’s macro lens mode and had to take many shots in soft light until reflection aligned.

WHY: I wanted to experiment with animal perspective and show how the world might be seen from another’s eye.

Solitary Glow

A candle burns alone in the darkness, symbolizing hope and quiet endurance.

Composition: Centered and minimal, reinforcing isolation.

Lighting: Low key lighting emphasizes warmth in dark tones.

Element of Design: Value—shadows define depth and mood.

Principle of Design: Unity—elements support a consistent emotional tone.

WHAT: A single wax candle placed in total darkness to stand alone in the frame.

HOW: I used my phone in manual exposure mode, turned off all lights, and stabilized on a desk for sharpness.

WHY: The image stands for quiet strength and the idea of internal energy glowing when nothing else is visible.

Sustainable Self

A portrait holding eco-tools to reflect conscious living and environmental identity.

Composition: Triangular composition with hand, face, and object.

Lighting: Soft front lighting gives clarity and natural tones.

Element of Design: Line—the bamboo straw points up toward the subject’s face.

Principle of Design: Balance—objects are visually equal across the frame.

WHAT: I posed holding a bamboo cup and reusable straw to share my beliefs in sustainability.

HOW: Taken with my phone using a timer and front camera with daylight from a window to keep it clean and natural.

WHY: I wanted the photo to act like an environmental ad, showing lifestyle choices visually.
